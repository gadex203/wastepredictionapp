## Tips (Examples)

1. Rinse containers: a quick rinse helps recycling systems work better.
2. Flatten cardboard to save space and reduce transport volume.
3. Remove food residue from paper; greasy paper often can’t be recycled.
4. Bottles and cans: empty them before putting them in recycling.
5. Batteries: never throw them in regular trash; treat as hazardous.
6. Glass: check for broken pieces; handle safely.
7. Keep recyclables loose (not bagged) unless local rules say otherwise.
8. Separate mixed materials when possible (e.g., remove plastic film from boxes).

---

## Myths vs Facts (Examples)

- Myth: “All plastics are recyclable.”  
  Fact: Recycling depends on type and local capability; some plastics are not accepted.

- Myth: “If it’s dirty, it’s fine.”  
  Fact: Food contamination can cause whole batches to be rejected.

- Myth: “Batteries are safe in the trash.”  
  Fact: Batteries can start fires and must be disposed of properly.
